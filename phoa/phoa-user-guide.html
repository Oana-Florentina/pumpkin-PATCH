Abstract

PhoA is a multi-device web application designed to help users manage and overcome phobias through personalized remedies, smart alerts, and group support. It combines evidence-based resources with semantic web technologies to deliver contextual and collaborative phobia management.

1. Getting Started

1.1 Registration

  1. Open the PhoA web application in your browser.
  2. Click Register.
  3. Complete the registration form with your email address and password.
  4. Submit the form.
  5. Check your email inbox and click the confirmation link to activate your account.

1.2 Login

  1. Return to the PhoA login page.
  2. Enter your registered email and password.
  3. Click Login to access the application.

3. Dashboard

3.1 Phobia Management Dashboard

The Dashboard is your main control center. Here you can:

  • View all phobias you are currently tracking
  • Add new phobias to monitor

For each phobia you can explore related remedies.

3.2 Add Phobias to Track

  1. Scroll to Add Phobias to Track.
  2. Use the search bar to find a phobia (e.g., spiders, confined spaces, mice).
  3. Select a phobia from the results.
  4. The phobia will now appear on your dashboard.

3.3 View Remedies & Resources

  • Click View Remedies & Resources on any phobia card.
  • You will be redirected to the Remedies page for that phobia.

4. Remedies Page

The Remedies page provides evidence-based support tailored to each phobia.

You may find:

  • Exposure therapy recommendations
  • NHS health guides
  • Breathing and grounding exercises
  • Media and educational references
  • Support community links

These remedies are powered by DBpedia and Wikidata knowledge bases.

5. Alerts Page

The Alerts page displays notifications related to your tracked phobias.

Alerts can be generated based on:

  • Group member reports
  • Context-aware conditions (location, time, weather, sensors)

This helps you stay aware of potential triggers in real time. Note, you need to enable notifications for this.

6. Groups Page

The Groups feature allows collaborative phobia management with family, friends, or coworkers.

6.1 Create a Group

  1. Enter a group name (e.g., My Family, Office Team).
  2. Click Create Group.

6.2 Invite Members

You can invite others by:

  • Sharing an invite link
  • Sharing the QR code displayed in your group

Members can join using the Join Group section.

6.3 Report a Trigger to a Group

  1. Enter a trigger message (e.g., "I saw a mouse in the house").
  2. Select the group.
  3. Send the alert.

All group members who track the related phobia (e.g., fear of mice) will receive an alert.

6.4 View Group Triggers

  • Click View Triggers within a group to see reported events.
